2D Computer Controlled Machining

Large Format Machining

For this assignment, I decided to make a simple step stool.

First, I modelled it out in Fusion. For the thickness, I used 14.5mm as it was what I had used previously

Next, I created the layout of the pieces to be exported as a DXF file to VCarve Pro. I added dogbones at this stage as it allows me to reduce the size of the dogbones, giving the stool a cleaner look

Next, I tried it out in VCarve Pro Trial to see how it would look like. After doing all the necessary stuff, this was the result

Next, I went to get it cut out. After everything was good, the cutting commenced

After cleaning up the area, I am left with my cutout pieces

After a little sanding for the edges, I am left with my step stool!

While I was satisfied by the final product, I reflected on what could be improved. One main issue I saw was that the step stool can be a bit wobbly and doesn't feel very stable when I am standing on it.. What I could have done was to add a back support for stability or made the steps longer, with the indents deeper, so that it would be more stable. However, I am still satisfied with the outcome.
